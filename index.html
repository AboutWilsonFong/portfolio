Inventory Control Report

A project completed for Logistics Supply Chain Management Course. Throughout the completion of this project, I acted as the leader for my group.

Letter Grade: A

Role: Formal Team Leader

Executive Summary

The purpose of this report is to analyze and make recommendations for the inventory control system currently utilized by the Country Hills Home Depot in Calgary, Alberta. An in depth analysis of Home Depot’s inventory processes, which is divided among three different processes: Manual, Electronic Data Interchange (EDI), and Vendor Managed Inventory (VMI) will be conducted. As well, an analysis of Home Depot’s communications and inter-store transfer system will also be conducted, followed by a number of potential solutions. Of the solutions, the promotion of communication and implementation of a standardized system is recommended, as well as updating the current electronic data interchange system. This can be achieved through the services of a third party such as JDA. This recommendation provides the most complete solution, recognizing improvements over every aspect of the current system. While this report is focused primarily on the Country Hills Home Depot, many of the issues discussed will be common among a number of other Home Depot locations. As such, the recommendations made in this report are not necessarily location specific and can be considered as potentially viable solutions to inventory management issues of the same nature at other Home Depot stores. It is also advised that Home Depot conduct further research, both in our recommendation as well in their competitors to consider the systems they use and if they may provide any improvements to the current system.

Conclusion for Inventory Control Report

Our final recommendation to improve the inventory management system currently utilized by Home Depot is to increase communication within the supply chain and standardize their inventory control processes through the implementation of revised forecasting methods and systems. Increasing communication within the supply chain will promote cooperation (Thomas & Griffin, 1996), both within stores and between stores, and will help to address larger issues related to performance and inventory itself.

By standardizing their forecasting techniques, Home Depot will see a reduction in the occurrence of over and understocking, as well as the Bullwhip Effect. This will benefit Home Depot greatly because, not only will the overall performance of inventory management system be improved, but the issue of holding costs associated with overstocking will also be addressed.

In standardizing their forecasting system, a third party program like JDA would provide a significant upgrade over the C3 system currently utilized by Home Depot. Of the packages JDA offers on their website, the inventory management, demand management, and supply chain planning packages would likely be the most effective to address the issues that we have discussed in this report.

JDA software will help improve Home Depot’s stock issues, specifically those related to inventory holes and understocking, through the adjustment of inventory policies and ordering strategies. JDA will further improve Home Depot’s forecasting accuracy with its integration of multiple forecasting methods into the framework it uses. These improvements will allow Home Depot’s forecasts to better reflect real time factors such as the market and/or customer demand, Team 3 Team Research Project Inventory Control 19 | P a g e as well as random variations in demand due to one-off events. Additionally, JDA will also assist in optimizing supply chain planning by reducing the amount of manual forecasting and planning currently required by department managers, ultimately reducing the risk of employee burnout (JDA, n.a.).

International Trade Management

This project was completed with a partner for my Forum for International Trade Training (FITT) Diploma. It required me to "take an existing or new product or service from the country of your choice into an international market which is new to the business". The research plan outlines a fictitious business entering a joint venture with Samsung, to develop a smart watch with a body fat analysis system.

Examination Results (May 22, 2018): 81/85

Executive Summary

The “Ubiquitous Environment” is a South Korean notion for how wireless technology is encroaching upon all aspects of daily living. The opportunity presented by this is clear in the fitness market, where consumers are embracing a growing trend of wearable activity trackers. As consumers take up greater interest in measuring health factors such as calories burned, steps taken, and resting heart rate, one significant feature remains to be seen in wearable products: body fat analysis.

NW Tech Inc. is a Canadian biotechnology firm specializing in portable body fat analysis. The company is poised to enter the activity tracker market by joining Samsung Electronics Co., the South Korean mobile technology giant, in a strategic alliance. Samsung Electronics’ Gear Fit activity tracker has been losing market share in China against competitors such as Xiaomi and Apple (Samsung Electronics, 2016). As a competitive strategy, Samsung Electronics regularly introduces new features to differentiate. By equipping the next edition of the Gear Fit with NW Tech’s body fat analysis technology, the strategic alliance will be first to market with the feature and regain lost market share.

As a market entry strategy, joining an established company in a strategic alliance offers tremendous advantages. NW Tech will benefit from Samsung Electronics’ brand loyalty, existing sales funnel infrastructure, and preferential access to key vendors including FOXCONN Technology Group and Samsung Electronics Logitech. This will allow the business to outsource competitively and with minimal costs to manufacture the units in Taiwan, export them to warehouses in China, and deliver them directly to customers in the major urban centres of Tianjin, Shanghai, Fujian, and Guangdong.

Using a price skimming strategy, the new Gear Fit NW will be marketed to affluent Chinese shoppers who are eager to obtain the latest consumer technologies and track their health and fitness goals. This growing demographic seeks foreign brands and is more willing to pay premium prices for luxury goods. The target market also prefers to buy online, as approximately 77% of activity wearables in China are purchased on the internet. By targeting the athletic, overweight, and middle-aged segments of this demographic with internet and TV advertising, the venture’s total market potential in China amounts to 207,350,000 customers with industry-wide sales of 35,000,000 activity trackers in 2017.

With an initial adoption rate of 1% of market share in the first year, Samsung Electronics’ forecasted revenue for the first year is approximately CAD 116,696,250. In exchange for NW Tech’s intellectual property, NW Tech’s primary revenue stream will be a royalty rate of 10% of the price of each Gear Fit NW sold. This will yield NW Tech revenue of CAD 11,669,625 and net income of CAD 884,408 in the first year. These figures, along with growth projections for subsequent years, demonstrate the direct exporting venture’s mutual profitability and that NW Tech should pursue it.

Conclusion for International Trade Manangement

The Gear Fit NW is a revolutionary product that allows bioelectric impedance analysis (BIA) technology to be much more accessible. As an activity wearable, the Gear Fit NW is unprecedently functional and fashionable.

Confidence can be drawn from the target market’s price inelasticity and habits. Affluent Chinese consumers are on the rise and regardless of economic standing, all consumers opt to attain foreign products. The elevated image that foreign products carry in the eyes of consumers makes price skimming strategies justifiable. The innovative Gear Fit NW is priced at a level comparable to competition such as Fitbit; consumers would only be paying 10% more. Additionally, with Samsung Electronics’ technology, battery life is prolonged which is a highly sought-after feature. Despite the added features, it costs less than what it would cost to create a Fitbit or Jaw Bone activity wearable. This makes the Gear Fit NW competitive and is certain to boost Samsung’s presence in China. The strategic alliance will take full advantage of Samsung Electronics e-HMS system which ensures the materials and quality of Samsung Electronics’ products are environmentally friendly (Samsung, 2008). This means the product can also cater to environmentally concerned consumers.

Large capital expenditures will not be needed because Samsung Electronics already owns preexisting capital. Additional brick and mortar capital is not needed given the emphasis on E-commerce. Furthermore, Samsung Electronics’ brand presence, expertise, and professional networking are fundamental to the success of this venture. Samsung Electronics Logitech has the capacity to carry out the strategic alliance’s distribution effectively and efficiently.

As seen in the financial statements of this business plan, forecasts show promising growth. Net income grows from CAD 884,408 in year 1 to CAD 4,319,343 in year 5. The profit margin increases from approximately 8% to 19%, with an average profit margin of 15% (see Appendix A). Additionally, there is an expected 32% sales growth in the second year of operations. The profit margin and sales growth provides additional confidence in the venture.

NW Tech has experienced and qualified staff that are more than capable of ensuring success. NW Tech’s management team has also taken precautionary measures to protect the firm from international business risks, such as attaining insurance from Export Development Canada. Given the opportunities and vast benefits in China, NW Tech can see promising growth and profits from this strategic alliance. Measures taken to mitigate risk ensures that NW Tech, alongside Samsung Electronics, is sufficiently prepared to take hold of the opportunities available in China.

Strategic Management: 72 Hour Midterm

For the course Strategic Management, the capstone course for my bachelor degree, I worked in a team of 5 to analyze Monarchia Matt International (MMI’s) and propose a recommendation within 72 hours. This assignment was designed to simulate a real life scenario where a team had to complete an assignment within a tight time limit, all while, developing a sound proposal. Notably, this assignment fulfilled the midterm requirement of the course.

During the 72 hours my team and I analyzed the business level strategy of MMI and conducted an external macro-environment analysis via tools such as PESTEL. Following, key opportunities and threats were identified. My team and I also utilized Porter's five forces and the VRIO framework. Altogether, the compiled analysis was drawn together to formulate a recommendation. To provide credibility and highlight the viability of the recommendation, key considerations such as pros and cons were outlined. The recommendation was complimented with an alternative strategy.

Recommendation

Based on the external and internal analysis we recommend MMI to continue to pursue their growth strategy by expanding and selling Hungarian wines.

After an internal analysis of the company it was determined that MMI’s “[biggest] issue is for Hungarian wines to be accepted in the US.” For MMI to overcome this weakness to the brand image, we recommend they maintain the current strategy of rebranding Hungarian wines. The company has taken action to “renaming blends to combat misconceptions” and receiving high ratings from independent experts to change social conception. The pro of this movement would be differentiation as one of the only Hungarian fine wine sellers and the capturing of a niche market where they overcome the negative preconception attached to Hungary. The US wine retail is the largest in the world allowing for large market potential. Additionally, the strength of MMI’s supply chain allows them ease of access to Hungarian wines. This helps limit the supply threat that is experienced by rival competitors while providing a sustained competitive advantage. The con is that if the cost of rebranding supersedes the revenue produced from Hungarian wine, it would be an inefficient strategy. Additionally, opportunities with other New World wines could be lost if the company exerts too much focus on Hungarian wine. However, given social complexity of the current management team and supply chain, such obstacles can be resolved.

After conducting an external analysis of the industry, there is an opportunity consistent with MMI’s current growth strategy. Taking into consideration of the demographics of Generation X and their tendency to opt for New World wines, it would be beneficial for MMI to have an online presence. Online presence will allow further marketing towards Generation X, Enthusiast, the Overwhelmed consumers of wine. Additionally, it would allow for a greater expansion of distribution sales. Pros of this strategy, this will allow MMI to continue rebranding Hungarian wine while expanding sales. Cons would include heavy capital expenditure for marketing and advertising initiatives and barriers such as state legislation for online sale.

Alternative Strategy

There is also an alternative strategy for MMI to pursue low cost leadership given their current supply chain control. This will allow MMI to produce a low cost wine that will compete more adequately against beverages as a whole for US consumption. It would also cater to the opportunity of growing popularity of $3 bottle of wine. However we do not recommend this strategy since MMI intends to maintain a high standard of quality and image of being strictly high quality and are not able to achieve economies of scale.
